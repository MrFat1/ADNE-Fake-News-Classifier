# ADNE: Fake News Classifier & Generator
Comparative study of Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Transformer-based models for fake news text classification. The repository benchmarks and compares a range of approaches — from classical ML baselines to transformer-based models — to classify news articles as real or fake.

Beyond classification, this project includes a **GAN (Generative Adversarial Network)** component that *generates* synthetic fake news articles. The architecture pairs a transformer-based **Generator** (which produces realistic-looking text) against a **Discriminator** (which learns to distinguish generated content from real articles). Training the two networks in opposition pushes the generator to produce increasingly convincing fake news samples.
 
This dual approach — both detecting and generating fake news — provides deeper insight into what linguistic patterns make misinformation believable, and can be used to augment training data for the classifiers.

## Results
Each notebook draws conclusions from the work performed within it.

To provide an overview of the project, the main results are summarized below:

* **EDA and Feature Analysis:** The topics and vocabulary used in each category of text differ significantly, as shown in the corresponding notebook.

* **Machine Learning Generator (Markov Chains):** Used for text and title generation with bigrams and trigrams. The generated text often formed grammatically complete sentences, however, it lacked semantic coherence.

* **Machine Learning Classifier (Logistic Regression):** Achieved 95% accuracy using only titles and 98% when using full news texts. However, when evaluated on text generated by a Markov model trained on "True" news, the classifier struggled to reliably determine whether the content was real or fake.

* 

* **Deep Learning Generators (GAN and Transformers):** The GAN showed poor semantic coherence in title generation, producing outputs that lacked meaningful structure, therefore we tried using a pre trained embeddings model, giving better results but still not believable news. On the other hand the transformers model was able to make better outputs, generating titles with semantic structure without needing much training.
